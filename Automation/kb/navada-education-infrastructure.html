The Three Roles Explained

Node B: HP Laptop (The Brain)
All services, all control
This is the brain. It runs everything: your Telegram bot, email responder, trading, WorldMonitor, Flix, voice commands. 14 PM2 services + 3 Docker containers.

All traffic from the outside world comes in through a Cloudflare Tunnel, which is an outbound-only connection from your laptop to Cloudflare's edge. This means your laptop has zero open ports facing the internet. Nobody can find it, nobody can attack it directly.
Node C: Oracle VM (Data + Disaster Recovery)
Monitoring, database, failover target
This is the database and monitoring layer. Elasticsearch, Kibana, Prometheus, Grafana, Uptime Kuma, Oracle XE all live here. It watches everything.

As of today, it's also your disaster recovery site with dormant copies of your critical services ready to wake up if the HP goes down. Think of it as a sleeping backup that springs to life when needed.
Node A: AWS EC2 (The Watchdog)
Health monitoring + failover trigger
Its only real job is to ping the HP every 5 minutes and ask "are you alive?"

If the HP doesn't respond 3 times in a row (15 minutes), EC2 SSHs into Oracle and says "wake up, you're in charge now." It's the independent referee that triggers the failover.

How They Talk to Each Other

All three nodes are connected via Tailscale, which creates a private WireGuard VPN mesh. Each node gets a stable IP (100.x.x.x) that works from anywhere, even if the physical IPs change. The encryption is ChaCha20-Poly1305, which is military-grade.

The Failover: What We Built Today

If your HP crashes or loses power, here's exactly what happens:

1
EC2 Detects HP is Down
3 failed pings x 5 min intervals = 15 minutes to detect
2
EC2 SSHs into Oracle VM
Runs failover-activate.sh over Tailscale
3
DNS Switches to Oracle
api.navada-edge-server.uk now points to Oracle's Cloudflare tunnel instead of HP's
4
Oracle Starts Critical Services
Telegram bot, inbox responder, trading API + scheduler wake up from dormant state
5
You Get a Telegram Message
"FAILOVER ACTIVE" - everything is running on Oracle now
When HP comes back online, EC2 detects the recovery and the entire process reverses automatically. State syncs back to HP, Oracle services stop, DNS restores. You get a "FAILBACK COMPLETE" message.
