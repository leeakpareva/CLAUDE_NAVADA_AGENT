What the Three Layers Do

SPF (Sender Policy Framework)
A DNS record that says: "Only these servers (AWS SES) are allowed to send email on behalf of navada-edge-server.uk." If someone else tries to spoof our domain, SPF fails and the email gets rejected.
DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail)
A cryptographic signature attached to every email. Gmail checks the signature against our public keys in DNS. If the email was tampered with in transit, DKIM fails. Our 3 DKIM keys prove every email genuinely came from us.
DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication)
The policy layer that ties SPF and DKIM together. It tells Gmail: "If an email claims to be from navada-edge-server.uk but fails SPF or DKIM, here's what to do with it." Also sends reports to Lee so we can monitor for spoofing attempts.
